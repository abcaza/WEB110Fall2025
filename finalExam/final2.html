Generations through the years

  1. First Generation (1967–1969): The very first Camaro came out as Chevy’s response to the Ford Mustang and it made a huge impact. It had that true American muscle-car attitude with bold styling, strong performance, and V8 options. This generation is still one of the most iconic today.

  2. Second Generation (1970–1981): This generation has a full redesign, making the Camaro longer, lower, and wider. Chevy leaned more into making the ride comfortable and being able to handle it instead of just raw muscle power. The convertible was removed, but the Camaro still kept its sporty feel while becoming more refined.

  3. Third and Fourth Generations (1982–2002):These generation made Camaro closer to the “sporty coupe” category rather than the classic muscle-car style. Emissions rules, fuel economy changes, and new technology made an impact on the design. Even though performance shifted, the Camaro stayed recognizable and continued to evolve.

  4. Fifth Generation (2010–2015): After being discontinued, the Camaro made a huge comeback. Chevy brought back a modern car with a retro-inspired look, mixing the best of both worlds: the classic design and modern engineering. This generation helped bring the Camaro back into the spotlight and reminded everyone why the Camaro name matters.

  5. Modern Era; Sixth Generation (2016–2024): The newest generation introduced a lighter, more advanced platform that improved handling and performance. Engine options ranged from a turbocharged 4-cylinder to V6, V8, and even supercharged V8 models. Whether someone wanted a practical daily driver or a serious performance car, there was a Camaro for almost everyone.

Trim Levels

Some trim levels focus on speed, while others focus on comfort or features. From the basic 1LT to the powerful ZL1, the Camaro offers these types of trims whether you want a car that’s easy to drive, packed with tech, or built for raw performance.

  1. 1LT (basic): entry-level, V6 engine which is basic but decent features.
  2. 2LT (comfort upgrade): has a nicer interior, adds more comfort like better seats (heated/ventilated) and dual zone climate.
  3. 3LT (tech and luxury): higher tech comfort, also based in the V6 engine, an upgraded infotainment, nicer audio and extra features.
  4. LT1 (first V8 performance): has a V8 engine for stronger acceleration and more power, keeping interior features simple.
  5. 1SS (sporty V8):has performance brakes, bigger wheels, and tires for aggressive driving experience.
  6. 2SS (power and comfort):also with V8 power, has luxury features like leather seats, heated/ventilated seats, and premium audio.
  7. ZL1 (top muscle performance): supercharged V8, track-ready suspension and brakes, for the ultimate fast and thrilling Camaro experience.

Engine Options

Base Engine: 2.0L Turbo I4

  • Horsepower: 275 hp

  • Torque: 295 lb-ft

  • 0-60 mph: ~5.4 sec

  • Good for everyday driving and fuel efficiency.

V6 Engine: 3.6L V6

  • Horsepower: 355 hp

  • Torque: 284 lb-ft

  • 0-60 mph: ~5.1 sec

  • Great choice if you want a sporty feel but still use it daily.

V8 Engine: 6.2L LT1 / SS

  • Horsepower: 455 hp

  • Torque: 455 lb-ft

  • 0-60 mph: ~4.0 sec

  • For strong performance seekers and it handles high speeds for faster acceleration.

V8 Supercharged: 6.2L LT4

  • Horsepower: 275 hp

  • Torque: 295 lb-ft

  • 0-60 mph: ~3.5 sec

  • Exremely fast and powerful. Not a good car for beginners!

In simple terms, the bigger the engine, the more power and speed the Camaro can produce. Smaller engines are easier to drive and more fuel-efficient, while larger V8 engines makes the car more famous muscle-car performance. No matter which engine you choose, the Camaro is still fun and exciting
